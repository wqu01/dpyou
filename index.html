For Presenters

As you are planning your PD, please refer to the Professional Development Rubric and Professional Development Lesson Plan. Please look through both to help guide you as you plan your session. While you do not have to complete the PD lesson plan, your planning and thinking should follow the model (e.g. What is the AIM? How are staff living the learning? What directions do you need to give staff so that they are equipped to do the work?) Please note that the feedback that you receive will be tied to these documents, as we begin to codify what we know works in PD.

A few other guidelines for you as you plan:

  • Remember that there will be staff from different schools, regions and grade levels. How does your session speak to them? While all of your sessions address topics that can help everyone, please remember that our staff is now much more diverse and in diverse contexts. Let's work to be clear!
  • Plan everything. Yes, everything. Think through timing, directions, and what you want teachers and staff to do in each moment of the PD.
  • Powerpoint slides should be visual anchors, not your script. Use less text in the powerpoint, more visuals---but do include your script in the notes section.

And some bottom lines; please:

  • Upload materials (powerpoints, handouts etc.) in the box folder HERE by EOD Monday, October 3rd. You should all have access. If cannot open the link, please do let me know ASAP. Please do so by naming your documents: TITLE_YOURNAME_TYPEOFDOC -Ex: RigorousQuestioning_Friscia_PPT or CourageousConversations_Roth_Handout.
