Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I sign up for sessions?

  • Starting October 15th, log into Sched to sign up for the sessions you would like to attend. We have over 60 DREAM Team staff sharing their best practices.

    Please make sure that you've downloaded Sched onto your smartphone! Directions can be found on the Sched tab. If you are having any problems, please first ask your school leader. If he or she can't assist, please contact Matthew Kautz at matthew.kautz@democracyprep.org.

  • What should I wear?

  • On Friday, we'll be in our DP YOU shirts and jeans. For Friday dinner, feel free to dress up a bit! On Saturday, business casual!

  • What should I pack?

  • The hotel has the basics such as towels, wash clothes, and those cute mini bottles of shampoo, conditioner, and body wash, but make sure to bring your own toothpaste, toothbrush, and anything else you think you will need to make your stay enjoyable.

    In addition to comfy rooms, the hotel also has a 24-hour gym and a pool that is open from 6am-11pm. Pack your swimwear or workout gear to take advantage of the amenities at the hotel!

  • How do we get there?

  • Schools will meet on their campus, and travel to the Downtown Marriott together in coach buses. Times will be staggered to ensure our arrival into Philly is as smooth as possible.

    Those individuals who wish to travel to the Downtown Marriott on their own will assume the cost of their own travel.

  • I have questions about travel and lodging. Who should I contact?

  • Each campus has an assigned point person that will field all questions. If you do not know who that is, please ask your school leader! This point person will be responsible for all travel and lodging.

  • What if I have any concerns with my room once I check in?

  • Any on-site concerns should first be directed to the campus point person. If that person can not address, please contact Amber Adams at amber.adams@democracyprep.org.

DP Festive!

By now you’re gearing up for DP YOU. You’ve scheduled your sessions, chosen your roommate, and selected your Philly adventures. There’s just one lingering question: what to wear-- especially since Friday evening calls for "DP Festive"? Not to worry, we've got you covered. Here's three tips to help you look your best Friday night.

1. DP Festive (adj) A thoughtful interplay of business and party—often, but not necessarily, with a pop of yellow; an outfit appropriate for work and happy hour.

2. Consider DP festive a notch down from cocktail attire. You aren’t required to wear a frilly dress or heels, or a suit and tie but you do want to look your best. Add some spark to your everyday teacher slay.

3. There will definitely be no uniform checks at the door. No tucked in shirt, gray slacks, black shoes and belts needed. Still the DP professional standard remains. Have fun with your look, but don't be THAT person.

For Presenters

As you are planning your PD, please refer to the Professional Development Rubric and Professional Development Lesson Plan. Please look through both to help guide you as you plan your session. While you do not have to complete the PD lesson plan, your planning and thinking should follow the model (e.g. What is the AIM? How are staff living the learning? What directions do you need to give staff so that they are equipped to do the work?) Please note that the feedback that you receive will be tied to these documents, as we begin to codify what we know works in PD.

A few other guidelines for you as you plan:

  • Remember that there will be staff from different schools, regions and grade levels. How does your session speak to them? While all of your sessions address topics that can help everyone, please remember that our staff is now much more diverse and in diverse contexts. Let's work to be clear!
  • Plan everything. Yes, everything. Think through timing, directions, and what you want teachers and staff to do in each moment of the PD.
  • Powerpoint slides should be visual anchors, not your script. Use less text in the powerpoint, more visuals---but do include your script in the notes section.

And some bottom lines; please:

  • Upload materials (powerpoints, handouts etc.) in the box folder HERE by EOD Monday, October 3rd. You should all have access. If cannot open the link, please do let me know ASAP. Please do so by naming your documents: TITLE_YOURNAME_TYPEOFDOC -Ex: RigorousQuestioning_Friscia_PPT or CourageousConversations_Roth_Handout.
  • For panels — I'd like the point person to upload your talking points and agenda.
  • Be prepared with your copies. Assume 30 people in each session.
  • Make sure you’ve communicated all tech and material needs to Matt Kautz!
